# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Future Foresight's owner and small internal team. They manage marketing content, creative production, publishing, leads, suppliers, customers, deals, tasks, and automation from a protected internal workspace. Most users are operators, not software specialists. They need to understand the current state and the next safe action without learning implementation details.

## Product Purpose

The Control Panel coordinates internal trade operations and the marketing production pipeline. For marketing, it guides a request from text generation through human review, optional image or video creation, final creative approval, and deliberate Facebook or Instagram publishing. Success means each item has one clear current state, one clear next action, visible automation progress, and no path to accidental or incomplete publication.

## Brand Personality

Professional, calm, dependable. The interface should communicate operational control and commercial credibility without feeling technical, decorative, or bureaucratic.

## Anti-references

- Dense developer consoles that expose job mechanics as the primary workflow.
- Generic dashboards made from repeated cards with equal visual weight.
- Interfaces that show every possible action regardless of state.
- Social scheduling tools that hide media readiness, approval state, or publishing risk.
- Decorative motion, novelty controls, and ambiguous icon-only actions.

## Design Principles

1. Show the next safe action, not every available action.
2. Model the operator's workflow, not the automation implementation.
3. Keep text, image, and video paths distinct after copy approval.
4. Make waiting, failure, review, approval, and publication states unmistakable.
5. Require human approval at content, creative, and publishing boundaries.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support keyboard navigation, visible focus, semantic status messaging, sufficient contrast, descriptive button labels, non-color status cues, responsive layouts, and reduced motion. Animation should be minimal and used only to communicate state changes.
